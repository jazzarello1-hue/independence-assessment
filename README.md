# Monthly Independence Assessment

A monthly assessment tool designed for teenagers (under 18) to track their progress toward independence and adulthood.

## Purpose

This assessment helps teenagers:
- Understand what their parents invest in them financially
- See how their contributions offset household costs
- Track progress in four key areas of independence
- Have meaningful monthly conversations with parents about growth

## How to Use

1. **Monthly Check-ins**: Take this assessment at the beginning of each month
2. **Review Together**: Parents and teenagers discuss results together
3. **Set Goals**: Identify areas for improvement in the coming month
4. **Track Progress**: Watch scores improve over time

## Assessment Areas

### Part 1: Life Skills & Daily Habits
- Personal hygiene and presentation
- Time management and sleep habits
- Physical health and fitness
- Academic/work responsibilities

### Part 2: Household Contribution
- Cooking and meal preparation
- Cleaning and maintenance
- Laundry and organization
- Outdoor and pet care

### Part 3: Financial Literacy
- Budgeting and expense tracking
- Saving and investing habits
- Understanding bills and insurance
- Smart spending decisions

### Part 4: Personal Discipline & Character
- Emotional regulation
- Communication skills
- Goal setting and follow-through
- Respectful living

## Philosophy

**For Teenagers:**
- Higher independence = Lower family costs
- Contributing = Learning valuable life skills
- Preparation for independent living
- "We're teaching you how to adult"

**Not Financial Burden:**
- Teenagers aren't expected to pay rent
- Goal is education, not exploitation
- Helps visualize real costs of independence
- Shows appreciation for parental investment
